Camera recapture protocol: 9.3 | move the camera and recapture the entire scene
Shot size: medium shot.
Camera: orbit 90.00 degrees to the right from the source camera position, eye level, roll 0 degrees with the source horizon as the zero-roll reference; target view is strict right-side view, eye level, azimuth +90.00 degrees, elevation 0.00 degrees. Keep the lens aimed at the same scene center as in the source image.
Space: move the camera and recapture the complete scene. Rebuild the perspective, scale, parallax, occlusion, and composition of the foreground, every object, midground, background, ground, walls, ceiling, and frame edges from the new camera position. Do not freeze the source background and rotate only a person, object, or local region.
Completion: the image moves with the camera. Infer and naturally complete every object structure and environment region that enters the new view frustum but was not captured in the source, using the source spatial relationships, environment style, materials, lighting, and colors. Content outside the new view may leave the frame.
Continuity: retain the identities and structures of the depicted elements, the same real-world moment, environment relationships, materials, colors, lighting, and overall style. Recompute the 2D projection, occlusion order, and background layout from the target camera instead of reusing those from the source image.
Acceptance: This must be a strict 90-degree right-side camera view: place the camera directly to the right of the scene center and look back toward that center. Any large projection that faced the source camera must become a visibly narrow edge projection, newly visible rightward depth must enter the frame, and the background must show matching lateral parallax. Use natural eye-level perspective without adding an unrequested high-angle or low-angle effect. For the medium shot, keep the scene focus and surrounding environment readable while preserving real spatial depth.
Reject: mirroring, 2D rotation, card flipping, perspective warping, cut-and-paste, ordinary outpainting, or changing only one object's angle without rebuilding the complete scene.
