# Single Image Camera Prompt Lab

This directory records the isolated prompt experiments used to validate the
production camera protocol. Both reference images have now passed all seven
target views, so the validated rules may be promoted to production.

## Reference images

- `image-1.png`: close portrait of a woman beside a window.
- `image-2.png`: white tabletop fan in a bright kitchen.

## Matrix

| View | Camera operation | Image 1 | Image 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Front | Source camera, XYZ 0, medium distance | pass v1 | pass v1 |
| Side | Horizontal orbit right 90 degrees | pass v1 | pass v2 |
| Top | Vertical orbit up 75 degrees | pass v1 | pass v1 |
| Bottom | Vertical orbit down 75 degrees | pass v1 | pass v1 |
| Wide | Dolly backward, no zoom substitution | pass v3 | pass v1 |
| Medium | Medium camera distance | pass v2 | pass v1 |
| Close-up | Dolly forward, no digital crop substitution | pass v1 | pass v1 |

## Global acceptance criteria

1. The source image is the sole source of identity, model, structure, material,
   lighting, scene, and readable markings.
2. The subject and scene remain fixed in world coordinates. The camera performs
   the requested orbit or dolly operation.
3. The complete image is re-rendered from the target camera. Source pixel
   coordinates and the original 2D projection are not preserved.
4. Subject silhouette, projected width, visible structures, occlusion order,
   foreshortening, and background parallax visibly prove the camera operation.
5. No subject turn-to-camera shortcut, 2D rotation, mirror, perspective warp,
   card flip, pasted subject, or unchanged background.
6. Category-specific geometry is required. Human anatomy is used only for the
   portrait; manufactured-part terminology is used for the fan.

## Side-view findings

### Portrait v1

Passed. The output forms a strict profile with one dominant eye, a silhouette
nose, far-side facial occlusion, and a reprojected hair/neck outline.

### Fan v1

Failed. The fan was mostly turned while the original kitchen composition was
retained. The grille remained too wide for a strict 90-degree view.

### Fan v2

Passed. The successful additions were:

- Explicitly discard all source-image 2D pixel coordinates.
- Keep the fan and scene fixed in world coordinates.
- Require grille projected width to be no more than 20% of its height.
- Reduce the front hub to a thin strip or occluded arc.
- Require front-to-back housing thickness and depth occlusion.
- Require strong, differential parallax across all kitchen objects.
- Declare "fan rotated, kitchen unchanged" an automatic failure.

## Front-view findings

Both images passed v1 when the source camera was treated as the zero-degree
camera and the complete frame was regenerated without adding an orbit.
Identity, structure, lighting, and scene layout remained faithful.

## Top-view and bottom-view findings

Both images passed v1 in both directions. Stable prompts required:

- A real vertical camera orbit with the optical axis aimed at the subject.
- No subject head tilt, product tilt, or local deformation as a substitute.
- A visible change in upper-versus-lower projected area and occlusion order.
- A corresponding change in horizon, background layering, and vertical
  convergence.
- For tabletop subjects, a low camera landing above the supporting surface so
  the camera does not pass through the table.

## Framing findings

### Wide

The fan passed v1. The portrait failed v1 and v2 because the person still
occupied roughly 70% of the frame. Portrait v3 passed after the prompt moved
the camera to a concrete far landing near the room entrance, defined the shot
as an environmental establishing view, and required the environment to occupy
about 80% of the frame.

### Medium

The fan passed v1. Portrait v1 remained a chest-up close shot. Portrait v2
passed after the prompt required a waist-up projection with both hands and the
chair back visible while retaining meaningful room context.

### Close-up

Both images passed v1. Stable prompts required a physical dolly-in with
stronger near-far scale differences, occlusion, background parallax, and depth
of field. Digital crop or uniform enlargement was an automatic failure.

## Promotion result

The validated protocol is recorded in
`camera-protocol-v0.3-validated-zh.md`. Its production rules are:

- Camera motion and landing are deterministic and precede image generation.
- Source pixels provide three-dimensional facts, not locked screen positions.
- The whole frame is reprojected after the camera reaches the target pose.
- Category-specific pixel evidence is generated by the reasoning model.
- Generic phrases such as "subject right surface" are rejected.
- Source screen orientation and source 2D projection are never locked.
